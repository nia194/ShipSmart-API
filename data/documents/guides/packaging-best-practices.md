# Packaging Best Practices

## General Rules
1. Use a box that fits the item with 2-3 inches of cushioning on all sides
2. Use new or structurally sound boxes — reused boxes lose 50% of their strength
3. Seal all seams with 2-inch packing tape (not masking tape or duct tape)
4. Remove or cover old labels and barcodes on reused boxes
5. Include a packing slip or address label inside the box as backup

## Fragile Items
- Wrap each item individually in bubble wrap or foam
- Fill all void space — items should not shift when the box is shaken
- Use "double boxing" for extremely fragile items: pack the item in a smaller box, then place that box inside a larger box with cushioning between them
- Mark the package as "FRAGILE" (though carriers do not guarantee special handling for this marking)
- Consider carrier-offered packaging services or specialty boxes
- Avoid using newspaper as sole cushioning — it compresses too easily

## Heavy Items (over 50 lbs)
- Use a double-walled corrugated box rated for the weight
- Reinforce the bottom of the box with extra tape (H-taping pattern)
- Distribute weight evenly inside the box
- Use internal supports or partitions for multiple heavy items
- UPS and FedEx accept up to 150 lbs per package; USPS maximum is 70 lbs
- Packages over 70 lbs may incur additional handling surcharges
- Packages over 150 lbs require freight shipping, not parcel service

## Oversized Packages
- Carriers define oversized as: length + girth > 130 inches (UPS/FedEx) or > 130 inches (USPS)
- Oversized packages incur significant surcharges ($30-100+ per package)
- Consider whether the item can be disassembled and shipped in smaller boxes
- Dimensional weight pricing will apply: large boxes with light items are charged based on volume, not weight
- The DIM factor is 139 for UPS/FedEx domestic and 166 for USPS

## Dimensional Weight (DIM Weight)
DIM weight is how carriers charge for large, lightweight packages. The formula:

**DIM Weight = (Length x Width x Height) / DIM Factor**

| Carrier | DIM Factor (Domestic) | DIM Factor (International) |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| UPS | 139 | 166 |
| FedEx | 139 | 166 |
| USPS | 166 | 166 |
| DHL | 139 | 139 |

The carrier charges whichever is higher: actual weight or DIM weight. To minimize DIM weight costs:
- Use the smallest box that safely fits the item
- Avoid excessive void fill that inflates box size
- Consider flat-rate options (USPS Priority Mail) for dense, heavy items

## Liquids and Gels
- Must be in leak-proof containers with sealed caps
- Wrap containers in absorbent material
- Place inside a sealed plastic bag
- Clearly label as containing liquids
- Check carrier restrictions — some liquids are prohibited via air

## Electronics
- Remove batteries if possible (lithium batteries have shipping restrictions)
- Use anti-static bags or wrap
- Surround with at least 3 inches of cushioning
- Consider shipping insurance for high-value electronics

## Clothing and Soft Goods
- Use poly mailers or padded envelopes for single items
- Plastic bags inside boxes to protect from moisture
- No special cushioning needed for non-fragile soft goods
- Poly mailers reduce DIM weight charges since they conform to contents
