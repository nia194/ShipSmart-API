# International Shipping Basics

## Overview
International shipping involves additional complexity compared to domestic shipping: customs documentation, duties and taxes, restricted items, and longer transit times. Understanding these factors helps avoid delays and unexpected costs.

## Customs Documentation

### Commercial Invoice
Required for all international shipments containing goods with commercial value. Must include:
- Sender and receiver full names, addresses, phone numbers
- Detailed description of each item (not just "electronics" — specify "wireless Bluetooth headphones")
- Quantity of each item
- Declared value of each item in USD
- Country of origin/manufacture for each item
- HS (Harmonized System) code for each item
- Total weight and number of packages
- Reason for export (sale, gift, sample, repair)

### HS Codes (Harmonized System)
- 6-10 digit codes that classify goods for customs purposes
- Required for all commercial shipments
- Incorrect HS codes can cause customs delays or incorrect duty charges
- Look up codes at hts.usitc.gov or use carrier classification tools

## Duties and Taxes

### Who Pays?
- **DDU (Delivered Duty Unpaid)**: Receiver pays duties and taxes at delivery. This is the default for most shipments. Receivers may be surprised by unexpected charges.
- **DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)**: Sender pays duties and taxes upfront. Better customer experience but more complex for the sender.

### De Minimis Thresholds
Many countries allow low-value shipments to enter duty-free:
- **United States**: $800 (one of the highest in the world)
- **European Union**: ~$150 EUR (varies by country)
- **Canada**: ~$20 CAD (one of the lowest)
- **Australia**: ~$1,000 AUD
- **United Kingdom**: ~$135 GBP

### Common Duty Rates
Duties vary enormously by product category and destination country. Examples:
- Clothing: 10-30% in many countries
- Electronics: 0-10% (often lower due to trade agreements)
- Food/beverages: Highly regulated, often restricted

## Restricted and Prohibited Items

### Commonly Restricted (require special documentation)
- Lithium batteries and devices containing them
- Perishable foods
- Plants and seeds
- Animal products
- Pharmaceuticals and medical devices
- Alcohol and tobacco

### Commonly Prohibited (cannot ship internationally)
- Flammable liquids and gases
- Explosives and fireworks
- Illegal drugs and narcotics
- Currency and monetary instruments (varies by country)
- Items sanctioned under export control laws

### Country-Specific Restrictions
Every country has unique import restrictions. Always check the destination country's customs website or carrier restricted items tool before shipping.

## Carrier Comparison for International

### DHL Express
- **Best for**: International express, especially to Europe and Asia
- **Strength**: Largest international network, customs expertise
- **Transit**: 2-5 business days to most countries
- **Customs**: DHL handles customs brokerage

### UPS Worldwide
- **Best for**: International shipments from the US
- **Options**: UPS Worldwide Express, UPS Worldwide Saver, UPS Standard (ground to Canada/Mexico)
- **Transit**: 1-5 business days depending on service and destination

### FedEx International
- **Best for**: Express international, especially documents
- **Options**: FedEx International Priority, International Economy, International Ground (Canada)
- **Transit**: 1-5 business days

### USPS International
- **Best for**: Budget international, small packages
- **Options**: Priority Mail International (6-10 days), Priority Mail Express International (3-5 days), First-Class Package International (varies)
- **Cost**: Typically cheapest for packages under 4 lbs
- **Limitation**: Tracking may be limited in some countries

## Tips for International Shipping
1. Always use the correct HS codes — guessing causes delays
2. Declare accurate values — undervaluing is customs fraud
3. Include a phone number for the receiver — customs may need to contact them
4. Consider DDP terms for better customer experience
5. Allow extra transit time (add 3-5 days buffer)
6. Use DHL for the widest international coverage
7. Check carrier websites for country-specific restrictions before shipping
