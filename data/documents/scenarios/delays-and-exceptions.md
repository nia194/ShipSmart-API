# Shipping Delays and Delivery Exceptions

## Understanding Delivery Exceptions
A delivery exception occurs when a package encounters an issue that prevents normal delivery. This does not necessarily mean the package is lost — most exceptions are resolved within 1-2 business days.

## Common Exception Types

### "Delivery Attempted — No Access"
- **Meaning**: Driver could not access the delivery location
- **Causes**: Gated community, locked building, closed business, road blocked
- **What to do**: Provide access instructions or schedule redelivery
- **Resolution**: Usually delivered on next attempt with updated instructions

### "Address Correction Required"
- **Meaning**: Carrier cannot match the address to a valid location
- **Causes**: Misspelled street name, wrong ZIP code, missing apartment number
- **What to do**: Contact carrier with correct address; may require new label
- **Resolution**: 1-3 day delay typically; package forwarded if correctable

### "Held at Facility / Available for Pickup"
- **Meaning**: Package is at local carrier facility
- **Causes**: Failed delivery attempts, recipient requested hold, customs hold
- **What to do**: Pick up at facility with ID, or schedule redelivery
- **Resolution**: Package held for 5-7 days before return to sender

### "Weather Delay"
- **Meaning**: Severe weather has disrupted carrier operations
- **Causes**: Snowstorms, hurricanes, flooding, extreme heat
- **What to do**: Wait — carrier will resume delivery when safe
- **Resolution**: Usually 1-3 day delay; no action needed from sender or recipient

### "Shipment Exception — Damaged"
- **Meaning**: Package was damaged during transit
- **Causes**: Insufficient packaging, handling errors, conveyor issues
- **What to do**: File a claim with the carrier; may need photos of damage
- **Resolution**: Claim processing takes 7-14 business days typically

### "Incorrect Address / Undeliverable"
- **Meaning**: Address is invalid and package cannot be delivered
- **Causes**: Address doesn't exist, moved with no forwarding, PO Box for carrier that doesn't deliver to PO Boxes
- **What to do**: Contact carrier to redirect or return
- **Resolution**: Package will be returned to sender if unresolvable

## What to Do When a Package Is Delayed

### Step 1: Check Tracking
- Look at the most recent tracking event and timestamp
- A "delay" of a few hours within the delivery day is normal
- If tracking hasn't updated in 24+ hours, there may be a transit issue

### Step 2: Identify the Delay Type
- **In transit delay**: Package is moving but slower than expected. Usually resolves on its own.
- **Facility scan delay**: Package was scanned at a facility but hasn't left. May be a sorting issue.
- **Delivery exception**: Specific issue preventing delivery (see above).

### Step 3: Contact the Carrier
- UPS: 1-800-742-5877 or ups.com/track
- FedEx: 1-800-463-3339 or fedex.com/tracking
- DHL: 1-800-225-5345 or dhl.com/tracking
- USPS: 1-800-275-8777 or usps.com/tracking
- Have tracking number ready
- For delayed packages: ask for "package research" or "trace"

### Step 4: File a Claim If Needed
- **Lost packages**: Most carriers consider a package lost after 7 days without tracking updates
- **Damaged packages**: Keep all packaging materials for inspection
- **Claim deadlines**: File within 60 days (UPS), 60 days (FedEx), 60 days (USPS), varies (DHL)
- **Required for claims**: Tracking number, proof of value, photos of damage (if applicable)

## Preventing Delays

### Shipping Best Practices
1. Ship early in the week (Monday-Wednesday) to avoid weekend cutoffs
2. Use address validation before shipping to catch errors
3. Drop off packages before the carrier's daily cutoff time
4. During peak seasons (November-December), add 2-3 extra buffer days
5. Use guaranteed services when delivery date is critical

### Peak Season Awareness
- November through January: holiday volume causes network-wide delays
- All carriers may suspend service guarantees during peak season
- Add extra buffer days for ground shipments
- Consider shipping earlier rather than upgrading to express (cheaper)

## Carrier Service Guarantees

| Carrier | Ground Guarantee | Express Guarantee | Peak Season |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| UPS | No | Yes (money-back) | May suspend |
| FedEx | No | Yes (money-back) | May suspend |
| USPS | No | Priority Mail Express only | May suspend |
| DHL | No | Yes (Express services) | May suspend |

Express service guarantees are typically suspended during severe weather, natural disasters, and peak holiday shipping season. Check carrier websites for current guarantee status.
