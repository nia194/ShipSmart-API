# Shipping Recommendation Tradeoffs

## How to Think About Shipping Choices
Every shipping decision involves tradeoffs between four factors: cost, speed, reliability, and value. Understanding these tradeoffs helps users make informed choices.

## The Four Factors

### Cost
- Ground shipping is always cheapest for non-urgent packages
- USPS is typically cheapest for packages under 5 lbs
- UPS/FedEx Ground are competitive for packages 5-70 lbs
- For packages over 70 lbs, only UPS and FedEx are options (USPS max is 70 lbs)
- Flat-rate options (USPS Priority Mail) can save money on heavy, small packages
- Residential surcharges from UPS/FedEx ($4-6) make USPS more attractive for home delivery

### Speed
- Next Day Air/Priority Overnight: guaranteed next business day
- 2-Day services: reliable for time-sensitive needs at moderate cost
- 3-Day Select/Express Saver: economy express option
- Ground: 1-5 days, varies by distance. Same-region ground can be 1-2 days
- USPS Priority Mail: 1-3 days at lower cost than UPS/FedEx 2-day

### Reliability
- Express services have money-back guarantees — ground services do not
- UPS and FedEx have the most consistent ground delivery times
- USPS Priority Mail is generally reliable but not guaranteed
- DHL is most reliable for international express
- Holiday season and severe weather can delay all carriers

### Value (Best Overall)
- "Best value" balances cost and speed for the specific shipment
- For most domestic packages: USPS Priority Mail or UPS/FedEx Ground
- For urgent packages: UPS 2nd Day Air or FedEx 2Day (cheaper than overnight)
- For lightweight packages: USPS Ground Advantage (cheapest option)
- For heavy packages: UPS/FedEx Ground (best rates for 50+ lbs)

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario: E-commerce Order (Consumer Expectations)
- **Customer expectation**: 2-4 day delivery
- **Best choice**: USPS Priority Mail (2-3 days, affordable, no residential surcharge) or UPS/FedEx Ground (if within 2-3 day zone)
- **Avoid**: Overnight unless customer paid for expedited

### Scenario: Sending a Gift for a Birthday
- **Key factor**: Must arrive by a specific date
- **Best choice**: Ship early with ground, or use 2-day/3-day express with guarantee
- **Tip**: Ship 7+ days early with ground to save money; use express only if time is short

### Scenario: Fragile or High-Value Item
- **Key factor**: Safety and insurance
- **Best choice**: UPS or FedEx (better handling infrastructure), add declared value/insurance
- **Avoid**: Cheapest option if it means less reliable handling
- **Tip**: Express services get priority handling which can reduce damage risk

### Scenario: Bulk Business Shipments
- **Key factor**: Cost per unit at volume
- **Best choice**: Negotiate rates with UPS or FedEx; use ground for non-urgent
- **Tip**: Mix carriers — use USPS for lightweight, UPS/FedEx for heavy items

### Scenario: International Shipment
- **Key factor**: Customs clearance and transit time
- **Best choice**: DHL Express for time-critical; USPS Priority Mail International for cost-sensitive
- **Note**: Factor in customs duties and documentation requirements

### Scenario: Last-Minute Urgent Shipment
- **Key factor**: Speed at any cost
- **Best choice**: FedEx First Overnight or UPS Next Day Air Early (delivered by 8 AM)
- **Backup**: Check if local same-day courier services are available
- **Note**: Check cutoff times — most next-day services require drop-off by 5-7 PM

## When to Prioritize Speed Over Price
- Legal or compliance deadlines (contracts, filings)
- Perishable goods (food, biological samples, flowers)
- Medical supplies or equipment
- Event-dependent items (trade show materials, wedding items)
- Customer satisfaction recovery (replacing a lost or damaged order)

## When to Prioritize Price Over Speed
- Routine business supplies or inventory replenishment
- Non-urgent returns or exchanges
- Bulk product shipments to warehouses
- Items with no time-sensitive end use
- Pre-orders or backorders where customer already expects delay
